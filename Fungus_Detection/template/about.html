About Our Project

Our Vision

Our vision is to revolutionize environmental health and safety through the integration of IoT and AI technologies. By creating a smart and efficient fungus detection system, we aim to improve air quality management in critical areas such as hospitals, ensuring healthier spaces for patients and staff alike.

How It Works

Our system employs a variety of sensors to monitor environmental factors that can lead to fungal growth. The DHT11 sensor tracks temperature and humidity, while the MQ7 and MQ135 sensors detect harmful gases that can signal poor air quality. The LDR sensor measures light intensity, and the BMP180 assesses pressure changes. Data from these sensors is processed through machine learning algorithms, which detect anomalies and issue timely alerts, enabling proactive measures against potential fungal infections.

Our Commitment

We are committed to continuous innovation and improvement. Our goal is to expand the capabilities of our fungus detection system to cover more environments and add additional layers of accuracy and predictive capabilities. We believe that technology can make spaces safer, and we are dedicated to pushing the boundaries of what's possible.

Why Fungi Are Dangerous

Fungi can pose significant health risks, particularly in indoor environments. They thrive in damp and poorly ventilated areas, and their spores can become airborne, leading to respiratory issues and allergic reactions. Certain types of fungi produce mycotoxins, which can be harmful or even toxic if inhaled or ingested. This makes it crucial to detect and manage fungal growth early to prevent health hazards and maintain a safe environment. Our system helps address these concerns by providing timely alerts and actionable insights, reducing the risk of fungal-related health issues.
